## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant, G., 1960** - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production: Foundational source-filter theory and acoustic parameters essential for understanding formant-based synthesis and vocal tract acoustics.

2. **Recasens, D., Espinosa, A., 2005** - Articulatory, positional and coarticulatory characteristics for clear /l/ and dark /l/: Detailed articulatory-acoustic mapping for lateral consonants including coarticulation patterns directly applicable to synthesis rules.

3. **Stevens, K.N., 1998** - Acoustic Phonetics: Comprehensive reference for acoustic-articulatory relationships and phonetic feature implementation in synthesis systems.

4. **Bladon, R.A.W., Al-Bamerni, A., 1976** - Coarticulatory resistance in English /l/: Key study on how /l/ resists coarticulation, foundational for understanding allophonic variation and formant transition modeling.

5. **Narayanan, S.S., Alwan, A.A., Haker, K., 1997** - Toward articulatory-acoustic models for liquid approximants based on MRI and EPG data: Advanced articulatory imaging and acoustic modeling techniques for lateral consonants providing detailed vocal tract configurations.
