## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Peterson & Barney (1952)** - Canonical vowel formant measurements from natural speech; essential comparison baseline for synthetic vowel targets.

2. **Chiba & Kajiyama (1941)** - Early foundational work on vowel nature and structure; historical reference for two-formant vowel synthesis.

3. **Cooper et al. (1951)** - Pattern playback methodology and technique development; essential for understanding the experimental instrumentation and broader research program on synthetic speech perception.

4. **Joos (1948)** - Acoustic phonetics framework; establishes the theoretical context that two formants can produce reasonable vowel approximations.

5. **Fletcher (1929)** - Early observations on single-formant vowel behavior in back vowels; provides historical context for perceptual averaging phenomenon.
