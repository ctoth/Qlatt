## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Hillenbrand et al. (1995)** - "Acoustic Characteristics of American English Vowels" - Essential reference for formant frequency data (F1-F4) across speaker types (men, women, children). Critical for establishing realistic formant targets in synthesis.

2. **Klatt (1980)** - "Software for a Cascade/parallel Formant Synthesizer" - Foundational paper for formant synthesis architecture. Directly relevant to Qlatt implementation.

3. **Peterson & Barney (1952)** - "Control Methods Used in a Study of the Vowels" - Classic canonical vowel formant measurements that remain the baseline reference for vowel synthesis targets.

4. **Fant (1970)** - "Acoustic Theory of Speech Production" - Foundational source-filter theory that underlies all formant synthesis, explaining VTL-formant relationships.

5. **Holmes (1983)** - "Formant Synthesizers: Cascade or Parallel?" - Directly addresses Klatt synthesizer architecture decisions about higher formant specification and their role in shaping overall spectrum.
