## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant et al. (1985)** - The foundational Liljencrants–Fant (LF) glottal flow model definition; essential reference for understanding the R-parameters (Ra, Rk, Ro) that are the focus of this work.

2. **Moore et al. (1997)** and **Rao et al. (2001)** - Provide the auditory perception model (excitation patterns, loudness) that underpins the perceptual distance measure used in this paper; critical for understanding how to map acoustic variations to perceived differences.

3. **Scherer et al. (1998)** and **Henrich et al. (2003)** - Prior empirical studies measuring JNDs for glottal parameters (Oq, Sq, asymmetry coefficient); provide direct comparison data for validating the present method's results.

4. **Klatt & Klatt (1990)** - Foundational work on voice quality variations in the Klatt synthesizer context; relevant for understanding how glottal parameters affect synthesis quality in formant-based speech.

5. **Veldhuis (1998)** - Analyzes spectral relevance of LF R-parameters; provides complementary spectral analysis perspective to the perceptual distance approach in this paper.
