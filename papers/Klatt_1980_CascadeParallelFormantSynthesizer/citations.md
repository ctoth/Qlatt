## Key Citations for Implementation

### 1. **Fant (1960) - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production**
   - Foundational acoustic theory underlying the cascade/parallel formant model
   - Essential for understanding vocal tract transfer functions and resonator design
   - Direct application to synthesizer architecture

### 2. **Gold and Rabiner (1968) - Analysis of Digital and Analog Formant Synthesizers**
   - Digital resonator design and impulse-invariant transformation
   - Core mathematical foundation for difference equation constants (A, B, C coefficients)
   - Directly referenced for digital filter implementation

### 3. **Stevens and Klatt (1972) - Current Models of Sound Sources for Speech**
   - Comprehensive treatment of voicing, aspiration, and frication sources
   - Fundamental for source generation architecture
   - Defines sound source types implemented in the synthesizer

### 4. **Flanagan, Ishizaka, and Shipley (1975) - Synthesis of Speech from a Dynamic Model of the Vocal Cords and Vocal Tract**
   - Articulatory synthesis contrast and comparison
   - Important context for formant synthesis approach
   - Relevant for vocal cord modeling and dynamic effects

### 5. **Holmes (1973) - The Influence of the Glottal Waveform on the Naturalness of Speech from a Parallel Formant Synthesizer**
   - Critical for understanding parallel vs. cascade tradeoffs
   - Direct implementation of parallel formant amplitude corrections
   - Formant frequency-to-amplitude mapping rules
