## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant (1960) - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production** - The foundational book on speech acoustics. Contains bandwidth formulas, formant theory, and much of the theoretical basis referenced here.

2. **Fant, Liljencrants, & Lin (1985)** - Original LF model paper. Already in project as `Fant_1985_LFModelGlottalFlow`.

3. **Gobl (1988) - "Voice Source Dynamics in Connected Speech"** - Studies temporal variation of LF parameters in running speech. Important for understanding how parameters change during coarticulation.

4. **Holmberg, Hillman, & Perkell (1988)** - Comprehensive glottal flow measurements for male/female speakers at different loudness levels. Provides empirical validation of the theoretical predictions.

5. **Sundberg & Gauffin (1979)** - Detailed analysis of glottal voice source waveforms and spectra, including singing voice characteristics. Relevant for understanding the relationship between waveform shape and spectral content.
