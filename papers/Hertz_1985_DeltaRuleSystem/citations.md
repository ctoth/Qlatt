## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **Hertz 1981 [3], 1982 [15]**: Earlier work on SRS (Synthesis Rule System) that directly informed Delta's design, covering text-to-phoneme rules and the limitations of purely linear representations
- **Allen et al. 1979 [2]**: MITalk system reference - one of the main competing synthesis rule development systems that Delta was designed to improve upon
- **Elovitz et al. 1976 [5]**: Classic letter-to-sound rules providing foundational approach to grapheme-to-phoneme conversion
- **Pierrehumbert 1981 [24], Anderson et al. 1984 [25]**: Foundational intonation synthesis work showing how f0 contours can be generated by rule
- **Fujisaki & Hirose 1982 [28]**: Alternative quantitative approach to prosody modeling using mathematical functions rather than phonological targets
