## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Stevens (1998) - Acoustic Phonetics** — Comprehensive reference providing acoustic-phonetic foundations for fricative synthesis; essential background for understanding formant-based approaches to fricative production.

2. **Shadle (1990) - Articulatory-Acoustic Relationships in Fricatives** — Directly addresses the connection between articulation and acoustic output for fricatives, providing essential context for understanding which acoustic properties are controllable in synthesis.

3. **Behrens & Blumstein (1988a, b) - Fricative Amplitude** — Early systematic work on fricative amplitude distinctions that established many of the foundational findings replicated and extended in this 2000 study.

4. **Forrest et al. (1988) - Spectral Moments Method** — Original methodology paper for spectral moments analysis applied to obstruents; foundational for the analysis techniques used throughout this paper.

5. **Hedrick & Ohde (1993) - Relative Amplitude Perception** — Complements this acoustic work with perceptual validation of relative amplitude as a cue to fricative place, directly supporting synthesis parameter selection.
