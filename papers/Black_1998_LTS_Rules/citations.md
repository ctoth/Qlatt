## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Daelemans & van den Bosch (1996)** [#6] - Alternative language-independent G2P approach; useful for comparing methodologies and understanding the state-of-the-art baseline this paper improves upon.

2. **van den Bosch, Weijters & Daelemans (1998)** [#12] - Demonstrates that joint phone+stress prediction outperforms modular approaches; directly validates the paper's stress assignment findings.

3. **Luk & Damper (1996)** [#8] - Stochastic G2P approach for comparison; represents an alternative statistical framework to the CART-based method presented here.

4. **Pagel, Lenzo & Black (1998)** [#11] - Companion paper on using LTS rules for lexicon compression; shows practical applications of the same framework for reducing TTS resource requirements.

5. **Breiman et al. (1984)** [#3] - The foundational CART book; essential reference for understanding the decision tree methodology underlying the LTS rule generation.
