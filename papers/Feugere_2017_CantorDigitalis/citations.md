## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Klatt 1980** (Reference 23) - Foundational cascade/parallel formant synthesizer architecture; directly relevant to Cantor Digitalis's hybrid formant synthesis approach
2. **Doval et al. 2006** (Reference 27) - Spectrum of glottal flow models; provides theoretical foundation for voice source parameter relationships used in Cantor Digitalis
3. **Henrich et al. 2005** (Reference 39) - Glottal open quotient in singing; essential for understanding the open quotient and asymmetry coefficient parameters used for voice quality control
4. **Fant 1960** (Reference 25) - Acoustic theory of speech production; foundational source-filter theory underlying the entire synthesis architecture
5. **Sundberg 2001** (Reference 31) - Singing formant; directly applicable to soprano and higher voice formant clustering techniques described in the paper
