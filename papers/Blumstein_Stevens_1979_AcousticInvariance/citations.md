## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant (1960) - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production**: Foundational text for understanding vocal tract acoustics and formant theory. Essential for any speech synthesis work.

2. **Stevens & Blumstein (1978) - "Invariant cues for place of articulation in stop consonants"**: The companion perceptual study that provides the theoretical basis for this paper's templates. Critical for understanding how burst spectra relate to perception.

3. **Zue (1976) - "Acoustic characteristics of stop consonants"**: Detailed acoustic analysis of stop consonant bursts. Likely contains spectral measurements and analysis techniques useful for burst modeling.

4. **Klatt (1976) - "A digital filter bank for spectral matching"**: Describes auditory-based spectral analysis that may provide better templates than LPC. Relevant to improving burst spectrum modeling in synthesis.

5. **Jakobson, Fant, & Halle (1963) - Preliminaries to Speech Analysis**: Classic work on distinctive features including the diffuse/compact distinction that underlies this paper's template approach.
