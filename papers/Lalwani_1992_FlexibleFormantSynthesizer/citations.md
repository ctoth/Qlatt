## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Klatt, D. H. (1980). "Software for a cascade/parallel formant synthesizer"** - The foundational paper that this dissertation extends. Essential reading for understanding the baseline architecture.

2. **Klatt, D. H., and Klatt, L. C. (1990). "Analysis, synthesis, and perception of voice quality variations"** - Describes KLSYN88 and voice quality modeling that this work builds upon.

3. **Fant, G. (1960). *Acoustic Theory of Speech Production*** - The theoretical foundation for source-filter models used throughout the dissertation.

4. **Fant, G., Liljencrants, J., and Lin, Q-G. (1985). "A four parameter model of glottal flow"** - The LF model that forms the basis for the new glottal source model in this dissertation.

5. **Holmes, J. N. (1983). "Formant synthesizers: cascade or parallel"** - Important comparison of synthesis architectures that informs the flexible architecture design.

6. **Childers, D. G., and Wu, K. (1990). "Quality of speech produced by analysis-synthesis"** - Related work from the same research group on speech quality assessment.

7. **Peterson, G. E., and Barney, H. L. (1952). "Control methods used in the study of vowels"** - Classic reference for vowel formant targets used in synthesis evaluation.
