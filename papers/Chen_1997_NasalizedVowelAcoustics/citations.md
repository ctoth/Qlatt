## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant, G. (1960). The Acoustic Theory of Speech Production** - Foundational work on acoustic theory including nasal resonances and formant theory; essential for understanding physical basis of nasalization effects.

2. **Stevens, K. N. (in press). Acoustic Phonetics** - Theoretical framework for how velopharyngeal coupling affects vocal tract acoustics; directly cited for predictions in this paper.

3. **Klatt, D. H., and Klatt, L. C. (1990). Analysis, synthesis, and perception of voice quality variations** - Directly relevant to synthesizing nasalized vowels and perceptual effects of nasal coupling.

4. **Maeda, S. (1993). Acoustics of vowel nasalization and articulatory shifts in French nasal vowels** - Comprehensive treatment of French nasal vowel acoustics; provides complementary analysis methodology.

5. **House, A. S., and Stevens, K. N. (1956). Analog studies of the nasalization of vowels** - Early foundational work establishing that F1 amplitude reduction is the primary acoustic cue for nasalization perception.
