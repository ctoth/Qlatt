## Key Citations for Follow-up

### 1. **Klatt, D.H. 1980. Software for a Cascade/Parallel Format Synthesizer**
   - Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, 67: 971-95
   - *Why it matters:* Foundational specification for the Klatt cascade/parallel synthesizer architecture that directly implements formant-based speech synthesis. Essential reference for understanding synthesis parameter control and acoustic modeling.

### 2. **Stevens, K.N. 1968. Synthesis of Speech by Electrical Analog Speech Production**
   - Acoustic Division of the Acoustical Society of America, 56: 603-7
   - *Why it matters:* Establishes acoustic-phonetic principles for how articulatory movements map to acoustic parameters, providing theoretical foundation for formant targeting and voice source control in synthesis.

### 3. **Peterson, B.D. and L. Malinson 1974. The Intelligibility of Synthetic Speech and Test Results**
   - Status Report on Speech Research, Haskins Laboratories
   - *Why it matters:* Provides empirical evaluation methodology and baseline naturalness/intelligibility metrics for assessing synthesis quality, directly applicable to evaluating Qlatt output.

### 4. **Flanagan, J.L. 1957. Note on the Design of Terminal Analog Speech Synthesizers**
   - Journal of the Acoustical Society of America, 29: 306-10
   - *Why it matters:* Early foundational work on terminal analog synthesis architecture, describing how cascaded resonators and sources combine to generate speech, establishing principles used in modern formant synthesizers.

### 5. **Rabiner, L.R. 1968. Digital Format Synthesizer for Speech Synthesis Studies**
   - IEEE Transactions of the Acoustical Society of America, 43: 882-8
   - *Why it matters:* Documents digital formant synthesis implementation including pitch-synchronous modulation and practical coefficient calculation, providing concrete algorithms for real-time synthesis systems.
