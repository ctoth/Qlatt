## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Klatt, D. H. (1980)** - Foundational cascade/parallel formant synthesizer specification that this paper builds upon; provides the core glottal area template used in their synthesis model.

2. **Schoentgen, J. (2001, 2003a, 2003b)** - Multiple papers on stochastic jitter models, shaping function models, and spectral models of noise in phonatory signals; directly addresses the theoretical framework for this jitter/noise synthesis approach.

3. **Titze, I. (2006)** - Myoelastic aerodynamic theory of phonation; provides the physiological foundation for understanding how aerodynamic conditions generate jitter and noise at the glottis.

4. **Story, B. H., Titze, I. R., and Hoffman, E. A. (1996)** - Vocal tract area functions from MRI; critical for the vocal tract modeling component (40-45 cylindrical sections) used in their synthesis pipeline.

5. **Yiu et al. (2002)** - Perception of synthesized voice quality in connected speech; demonstrates that listeners evaluate synthetic voice quality similarly to natural speech, validating the relevance of their dysphonia synthesis corpus.
