## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Holmes, Mattingly, & Shearme (1964) - Speech synthesis by rule** - Early formant synthesis work establishing target-and-transition model used throughout Hertz's paper

2. **Fant (1960) - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production** - Foundational acoustic phonetics providing the physical basis for formant-based synthesis models

3. **Pierrehumbert (1980) - English Intonation** - Detailed F0 contour modeling directly relevant to prosody generation in Bambara and English examples

4. **Clements & Keyser (1983) - CV Phonology** - Multi-level representation theory that Delta implements, fundamental to understanding non-linear phonological structure

5. **Keating (1985) - Universal phonetic properties** - Coarticulation and phonetic detail relevant to Hertz's formant transition models
