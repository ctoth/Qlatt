## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Banse & Scherer (1996)** - Acoustic profiles in vocal emotion expression. Provides comprehensive acoustic parameter measurements (F0, intensity, spectral distribution) for 14 emotions, directly enabling implementation of emotion-specific acoustic targets in synthesis systems.

2. **Williams & Stevens (1972)** - Emotions and speech: Some acoustical correlates. Foundational work establishing systematic relationships between emotional expression and acoustic parameters; essential for understanding the physical basis of emotional prosody.

3. **Scherer (1986)** - Vocal affect expression: A review and a model for future research. Comprehensive theoretical model linking physiological emotion changes to acoustic parameter modifications; provides the explanatory framework for emotion-specific synthesis design.

4. **Ekman (1982)** - Emotion in the human face. Seminal reference on cross-cultural emotion recognition; establishes the broader context for understanding universality versus cultural specificity in emotional expression across modalities.

5. **Murray & Arnott (1993)** - Toward a simulation of emotion in synthetic speech: A review of the literature on human vocal emotion. Direct application to speech synthesis; reviews methods for embedding emotional expression in synthesized speech with parameter recommendations.
