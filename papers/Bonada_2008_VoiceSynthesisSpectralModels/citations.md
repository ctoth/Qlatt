## Key Citations for Follow-up

### Most Relevant for Speech Synthesis Implementation

1. **Klatt, D.H. (1980) - "Software for a cascade/parallel formant synthesizer."** Journal Acoustics of American Society, pp. 971-995.
   - Essential reference for understanding the cascade/parallel formant synthesis architecture that underpins modern Klatt-style systems like Qlatt. Provides the fundamental digital resonator equations and parameter specifications.

2. **Serra, X. (1989) - "A System for Sound Analysis-Transformation-Synthesis based on a Deterministic plus Stochastic Decomposition."** PhD thesis, Stanford University.
   - Foundational work on spectral modeling and source-filter decomposition. Bonada extends this framework for singing voice synthesis and it directly informs spectral processing techniques used in formant synthesis.

3. **Fant, G. (1986) - "Glottal flow: models and interaction."** Journal of Phonetics, vol. 14, pp 393-399.
   - Core theoretical reference for glottal source modeling. Critical for understanding how to control voice quality and source characteristics in the Klatt synthesizer.

4. **Rodet, X., Y. Potard, and J.B. Barrière (1984) - "The CHANT Project: from the Synthesis of the Singing Voice to Synthesis in General."** Computer Music Journal, 8(3):15-31.
   - Seminal work in formant-based singing synthesis. Demonstrates practical implementations of cascade formant synthesis and provides performance-driven control strategies.

5. **Bonada, J., A. Loscos, P. Cano, X. Serra, and H. Kenmochi (2001) - "Spectral Approach to the Modeling of the Singing Voice."** Proceedings of the 111th AES Convention.
   - Bonada's own work synthesizing the spectral modeling approach with classical formant synthesis, demonstrating how Klatt-based architectures can be enhanced with modern spectral techniques for improved naturalness.
