## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Klatt, D. H. (1976b). Linguistic uses of segmental duration in English.** - Contains the complete duration model referenced throughout, essential for TTS timing rules.

2. **Blumstein, Stevens & Nigro (1977). Property detectors for bursts and transitions.** - Describes acoustic-phonetic invariants that can inform synthesis target selection.

3. **Zue, V. W. (1976). Acoustic characteristics of stop consonants.** - Detailed measurements of stop consonant acoustics useful for Klatt parameter settings.

4. **Lowerre & Reddy (1978). The HARPY speech understanding system.** - Describes the 15,000-state decoding network that LAFS improves upon.

5. **Chomsky & Halle (1968). The Sound Pattern of English.** - Foundational phonological rules that should be implemented in TTS frontend.
