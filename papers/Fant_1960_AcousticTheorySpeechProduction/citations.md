## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant, C. G. M. (1952)** - "Transmission properties of the vocal tract with application to the acoustic specification of phonemes". This is Fant's own M.I.T. technical report that forms the core of the acoustic theory presented in this book.

2. **Delattre, P. C., Liberman, A. M., and Cooper, F. S. (1951, 1955)** - Multiple papers on synthetic vowels, acoustic loci, and transitional cues for consonants. These are key experimental validations of the acoustic theory with pattern playback.

3. **van den Berg, Jw. (1950s)** - Multiple papers on vocal fold mechanics, subglottic pressure, and laryngeal function. Essential for understanding the physiological basis of the voice source model.

4. **Cooper, F. S., Delattre, P. C., Liberman, A. M., Borst, J. M., and Gerstman, L. J. (1952)** - "Some experiments on the perception of synthetic speech sounds". Foundational perception studies validating formant synthesis.

5. **Peterson & Barney (implied in Fant's measurements)** - Though not explicitly listed as a full citation in this bibliography, the canonical vowel formant targets derive from Peterson & Barney (1952) measurements that underpin all vowel synthesis parameters in the book.
