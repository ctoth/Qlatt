## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **House & Stevens (1956)** - Foundational analog synthesizer study of vowel nasalization using electrical circuits; provides the original empirical basis for acoustic correlates of nasality.

2. **Fant (1960)** - *Acoustic Theory of Speech Production*; establishes the source-filter model and transfer function theory underlying all formant-based synthesis including pole-zero pair manipulation.

3. **Klatt (1980)** - The cascade/parallel formant synthesizer specification; directly implements the synthesis architecture used in this paper's experiments.

4. **Delattre (1954, 1968)** - Early pattern-playback synthesis studies showing that pole-zero pairs can modify vowel spectra; precursor to modern Klatt-based nasal synthesis.

5. **Maeda (1982a, 1982b)** - Competing acoustic hypothesis for nasalization based on sinus cavity coupling; provides alternative physical model to compare against pole-zero approach.
