## Key Citations for Follow-up

- **[3] Klatt 1980** - The seminal paper on cascade/parallel formant synthesis; defines the complete synthesizer architecture that this implementation extends. Essential reference for understanding parameter ranges and filter configurations.

- **[2] Fant, Liljencrants, Lin 1985** - Describes the LF (Liljencrants-Fant) glottal source model with four independent parameters (tp, te, ta, Ee). This is the glottal source model used throughout the implementation.

- **[1] Fant 1960** - Foundational acoustic theory of speech production showing source-filter decomposition. Provides the theoretical basis for formant synthesis architecture.

- **[4] Klatt & Klatt 1990** - Addresses voice quality variations and analysis/synthesis/perception studies. Relevant for understanding how parameter settings affect perceived voice characteristics.

- **[6] Holmes 1983** - Research comparing cascade vs. parallel formant synthesis configurations. Directly addresses the architectural choice implemented in this system.
