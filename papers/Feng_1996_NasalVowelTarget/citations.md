## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Fant, G. (1960) - Acoustic Theory of Speech Production** - Foundational work on vocal tract modeling and area functions; provides the theoretical basis for transmission line models used throughout this paper.

2. **Hawkins, S., and Stevens, K. N. (1985) - Acoustic and Perceptual Correlates of the Non-Nasal-Nasal Distinction** - Directly addresses nasality perception and establishes perceptual validation criteria referenced in this work.

3. **House, A. S., and Stevens, K. N. (1956) - Analog Studies of the Nasalization of Vowels** - Seminal work on nasal vowel acoustic properties that this paper builds upon and improves.

4. **Maeda, S. (1982b, 1984, 1993)** - Series of papers on nasal vowel acoustics, particularly regarding spectral peaks and sinus cavity effects; directly relevant to the target hypothesis.

5. **Lindqvist-Gauffin, J., and Sundberg, J. (1976) - Acoustic Properties of the Nasal Tract** - Provides sweep-tone measurements crucial for validating the pharyngonasal tract model.
