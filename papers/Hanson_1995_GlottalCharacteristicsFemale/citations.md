## Key Citations for Follow-up

1. **Klatt, D., and Klatt, L. (1990)** - Directly relevant for voice quality variations and parameter control in female speakers, establishing foundational metrics (H1-H2) for formant synthesis systems.

2. **Stevens, K. N., and Hanson, H. M. (1995)** - Co-authored with Hanson herself, provides methods for classifying glottal vibration from acoustic measurements central to the dissertation's findings.

3. **Fant, G. (1960, 1962, 1972, 1993)** - Foundational voice source modeling and formant theory essential for implementing any formant synthesizer architecture.

4. **Titze, I. R. (1989a, 1989b)** - Establishes physiologic and acoustic differences between male and female voices, directly relevant to sex-specific synthesis parameter selection.

5. **Gobl, C. and Ní Chasaide, A. (1988, 1993)** - Provides methods for analyzing voice source variation and consonant-dependent effects on voice quality, essential for contextual parameter modification in synthesis.
